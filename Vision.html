My Vision & Dream

My vision for life may sound smooth when spoken about, but achieving it is not easy especially if laziness comes into play.

I come from a family where many members work in the mining sector. I have witnessed firsthand how a small mineral can change someone’s life, lifting them to new heights. Yet, I have also seen the devastating consequences when things collapse: lives are lost, and our country loses valuable resources.

One day, while studying geology, our teacher emphasized the importance of the natural resources in the DRC. From that day, I began to reflect deeply on what I wanted to pursue at university. That’s how my passion for mineralogy and the mining sector started. I wanted to find ways to improve how resources are used and to make sure they benefit our communities.

Since my high school years, I have got to know that many of the technological assets that we use today evolve thanks to the mineral resources of the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC); This truth makes me feel both guilty and deeply motivated. Guilty, because our country holds the keys to global innovation but has not yet unlocked its full potential. Motivated, because I carry within me a strong spirit a burning desire to contribute to changing this narrative. known not only for its minerals, but also for the intelligent systems, innovations, and technologies created by its own people.

My passion lies in innovation and technology. My vision is to see the DRC become a strong exporting country where opportunities are limitless. We cannot rely on others to develop our nation for us. I want to see poverty reduced, and our youth empowered. Too often, those who study hard still watch outsiders enjoy our wealth, while local youth struggle, sometimes being forced to work with the army.
